A (short) story (about magic courgettes, bits in red are edited, original here):

Long, long ago, in a land full of wonderful history, a wise farmer owned a grand field of rare courgettes, which he would not give away. These courgettes were no ordinary vegetables, whenever you ate them, they made you stronger than a wrestling rhino.

Sneaking around like a spy under cover, the grand Imperial guards overheard the distressed Emperor pleading for the rare and wonderful courgettes. Wishing for great strength, the Emperor trudged angrily out of his room.

After hearing the Emperor's private thoughts, the two insane Imperial guards crept out of the golden temple and stole the precious courgettes.

The frustrated Emperor was so furious that he ran like a rocket to the farmer's wooden house, begging for forgiveness.

This is why forgiveness is important and stealing is wrong. Why, because the Emperor says so?

I wrote this story in primary school, but I can't remember at what age. In case you're wondering, a courgette is the proper name for a 'zucchini', and no, I don't believe I have ever eaten one.
