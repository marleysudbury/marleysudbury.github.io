Important question: what is the best text editor? Well, it depends on what you are doing... Notepad++ is good if you're on Windows, and Brackets is really nice because it has the Live Preview mode for use with the Google Chrome browser. It is, in my opinion, a Dream Weaver killer, although I personally had to spend a long time customising it. Basically it comes down to lightweight vs. functionality. Take your pick, as long as you dont pick Sublime, like, I would totally rather you used VIM than that garbarge. IDEs? Ain't nobody got time for that. Unless it's C.

Mac vs. PC: Well, I own both, so I don't have a massive amount of preference either way. Working on a PC is easier because, contrary to popular belief, they have a far greater tedency to 'just work'. A Mac, with a small number of choice apps and files, works just fine, but there is a significantly smaller selection of things you can do.
