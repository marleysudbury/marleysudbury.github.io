A Sequence From the Past 20/05/2022

Gobbledygook

That's a hard word to spell. Today I was looking ahead in my calendar to get a feel for how busy the next few months would be. Whilst doing so, I saw an entry that was utterly incomprehensible.

My initial reaction to this was that Siri must have listened to me discussing programming with someone, and mistaken it for an instruction to create an event. However, that answer didn't sit right with me. The words were too well formed. If you want to work it out yourself, this is your last chance.

Glimmer of recognition

The word Sequence stirred something deep within me. I recall that at some point I was quite fond of taking pairs of numbers and turning them into a sequence like the Fibonacci sequence. And since here there was a pair of numbers, that seemed like a sensible place to start.

I put together a quick Python program to do the calculations for me.

When run, lo and behold:

The sixteenth integer in the sequence is quite clearly 60822, which can be interpreted as 06/08/2022. However, this doesn't answer all of the questions.

Memory Blank

I have no recollection of this particular sequence, nor of the significance of its starting pair. I also don't remember adding this to my calendar. Luckily, at least on that last point there is some evidence left behind. By examining the .ics file in a text editor, we can see the date on which it was created.

Here though, the trail goes cold. Will something strange happen on that day? Probably. Will I ever understand the significance of those number or the sequence they generate? Probably not. When I checked my camera roll for 17/05/2017 I found two photographs of fidget spinners and the SCP logo.
